A2. Who we are and what we stand for

Introduction to our 57th Annual Report

Purpose of this Report: This Annual Report explains how Commercial Bank of Ceylon PLC, its subsidiaries and the associate (“the Group”) create and protect value over time, and how the Board and the Management steward the Bank through evolving economic, regulatory and sustainability landscapes. It is designed to help investors, depositors, lenders, regulators and other stakeholders assess our performance, financial position and resilience, while providing an understanding of the strategy, risks and sustainability-related aspects that may influence future outcomes.

We apply a decision-usefulness lens throughout the Report and prioritise information that can reasonably influence assessments of performance, resilience and stewardship.

What is new in 2025: The 2025 Annual Report includes our inaugural sustainability-related Financial Disclosures under SLFRS S1 and SLFRS S2, reflecting the Group’s adoption of the Sri Lankan equivalents of the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards. These disclosures are intended to provide decision-useful information on sustainability and climate-related risks and opportunities that could reasonably be expected to affect the Group’s cash flows, access to finance and cost of capital over time. In this first year of adoption, the disclosures include transition and implementation reliefs where permitted; consequently, comparative sustainability information is not presented.

Reporting period, boundary, and scope: This Report covers the financial year from January 01, 2025 to December 31, 2025, with comparative information for the prior year where relevant and available. The Audited Financial Statements are prepared on a consolidated basis for the Group in accordance with Sri Lanka Accounting Standards and applicable regulatory requirements. Sustainability-related disclosures primarily focus on the Group’s operations in Sri Lanka and its material overseas operations, and on sustainability-related and climate-related risks and opportunities that could affect the Group’s financial performance and resilience. There were no changes in the reporting or restatements to the previously reported financials (except for those disclosed in the Note 69 of the Notes to the Financial Statements on page 470), social or environmental information.

Integrated reporting framework and standards: This Report has been prepared in line with the principles of the International <IR> Framework, reflecting the Group's commitment to transparent, coherent and comparable reporting. It also aligns with applicable financial reporting standards, corporate governance requirements and relevant sustainability reporting frameworks. In developing sustainability-related disclosures, the Bank has considered evolving global best practices and regulatory developments to enhance the relevance, consistency and robustness of information provided to stakeholders, particularly on Climate-Related Risks and Opportunities (CRROs) that may affect long-term value creation.

Integrated Thinking for Sustainable Value Creation: Integrated thinking connects the Bank’s purpose, strategy, governance, culture and performance into a unified framework for decision-making. By considering financial and non-financial factors together, the Bank strengthens its ability to respond to opportunities and risks while delivering sustainable value to its stakeholders.

Materiality and stakeholder inputs: Material matters disclosed in this Report reflect the Bank’s assessment of issues that could reasonably influence stakeholder decisions and/or represent significant impacts. Stakeholder engagement informs both our materiality assessment and how we prioritise disclosures. Through structured dialogue with customers, employees, investors, regulators and community stakeholders, we identify emerging expectations and refine reporting to remain relevant, balanced and decision-useful.

Our approach to materiality: We apply a double materiality perspective by considering both financial materiality (sustainability-related risks and opportunities that could reasonably be expected to affect the Bank's prospects) and impact materiality (the Bank’s significant impacts on people and the environment, including through financing activities and operations). SLFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards (2025) alignment: Sustainability-related financial disclosures prepared under SLFRS S1/S2 are based on financial materiality.

Forward-looking statements and key judgements: Certain information in this Report is forward-looking and reflects management’s current expectations, assumptions and judgements, including in relation to strategy, risk outlook and climate-related analysis. Actual outcomes may differ due to changes in economic conditions, regulations, market dynamics and other factors.

About Commercial Bank of Ceylon PLC

Organisational overview: Commercial Bank of Ceylon PLC is Sri Lanka’s largest private sector bank and the only private sector bank designated as a Higher-Tier Domestic Systemically Important Bank (D-SIB) by the CBSL. As the leading and most awarded private sector bank, we serve more than four million customers through four core business segments (Personal Banking, Corporate Banking, Treasury and International Operations), delivered via a nationwide network of 272 branches and continuously enhanced digital channels. While Sri Lanka is our primary market, the Bank also maintains a regional presence in Bangladesh with a network of 20 branches, while the Group has a presence in the Maldives (a subsidiary), Myanmar (a subsidiary), and a representative office in the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC).

Core franchise and market position: The Bank operates one of the largest and most diversified banking franchises in Sri Lanka, serving retail customers, SMEs, large corporates and public sector institutions. Key highlights include leadership positions by assets, loans and deposits; being the first private sector bank to exceed Rs. 2 Tn. in assets, deposits and loans; being the largest lender to the SME sector since 2020; and achieving market capitalisation of USD 1 Bn., the highest among CSE-listed financial institutions.

International diversification: International expansion has been deliberate and selective, guided by risk-return considerations. Overseas operations are a material component of the Group, accounting for 15.72% of consolidated assets and 30.93% of consolidated profit before taxes.

A4. Operating context and stakeholder landscape

Operating environment and outlook

Global economy 2025: The global economy remained more resilient than expected, continuing to expand despite major shifts in the global trading system, heightened policy uncertainty, and ongoing geopolitical tensions. Global growth is projected at around 3.3% in 2026.

Sri Lankan economy 2025: GDP grew by 5.0% between January – September 2025. Inflation remained subdued in 2025, with headline inflation negative in early months before gradually moving toward positive territory. The CBSL maintained an easing monetary policy stance, reducing the overnight policy rate by 25 basis points in May 2025. Credit to the private sector accelerated markedly in 2025. The Sri Lankan rupee depreciated by 5.9% against the US dollar in 2025.

The Bank’s performance compared to the Banking sector: The Bank outperformed the sector in key metrics such as ROA (3.56% vs 2.59% before tax), ROE (22.06% vs 17.39%), Net Interest Margin (4.27% vs 4.35%), CASA ratio (38.07% vs 31.23%), and Net Stage 3 loans to total loans (2.76% vs 5.64%).

Stakeholder engagement: The Bank recognises that sustainable long-term value creation is fundamentally driven by meaningful stakeholder engagement. Our stakeholders, as providers of the financial, human, intellectual, social and natural capitals that enable our operations, play a vital role in shaping our strategy, performance and long-term resilience.

Our stakeholder engagement principles: Accessibility, constructive engagement, transparency, ethical governance, responsiveness.

How we engage: Through formal meetings, industry forums, surveys, digital platforms, social media and other feedback mechanisms.

Material matters: Material matters are the issues that can reasonably be expected to influence the decisions of our report users and/or affect the Bank’s ability to create value over the short, medium and long term. Our approach is informed by the principle of decision usefulness. We present our material matters through six Thematic Material Matters: 1) Risk, compliance and governance; 2) Financial performance and economic contribution; 3) Customer centricity and experience; 4) Innovation and digital transformation; 5) Sustainability; 6) People and community.

Our Business Model and the Sustainability Framework are closely integrated. The model transforms six types of capital (Financial, Manufactured, Intellectual, Human, Social & Relationship, Natural) into sustainable value for stakeholders through value-generating activities (offering omni-channel, relationship-based operations, funding and investments, financial intermediation, transactional banking, fee-based operations, products and services). These activities are driven by strategic imperatives: Sustainable market leadership & growth, Pervasive digital transformation, Unrivalled customer centricity, Talent & culture excellence, and ESG integration & responsible banking.

A7. Embedding sustainability into operations

Bank’s Sustainability Framework: The Framework is organised around three interconnected pillars: Sustainable Banking, Responsible Organisation, and Community Engagement. It aligns closely with the Bank’s Business Model and is applied across the entire value chain.

Sustainable banking: Responsible financing, financial inclusion, and sustainable products & services. Key achievements include a Rs. 71.2 Bn. green financing portfolio, the issuance of a Rs. 15 Bn. Green Bond, and being the largest SME lender for the 5th consecutive year.

Responsible organisation: Green & safe workplace, workplace culture, and sustainable supply chain. Key initiatives include renewable energy adoption (18.11% of energy consumption from solar), waste management, employee training (over 192,000 hours), and diversity & inclusion.

Community engagement: Environmental engagement, social engagement, and social enterprise. Key initiatives include the "Trees for Tomorrow" reforestation programme, establishment of IT labs (429 total), STEM classrooms (195 total), and disaster relief efforts.

A8. Analysis of financial performance

Financial review: For the financial year 2025, the Group achieved net profits of Rs. 60.938 Bn. (+9.43%) and the Bank achieved Rs. 58.487 Bn. (+8.16%). When measured against normalised FY2024 earnings (adjusted for the one-off accounting impact of the SLISB restructuring), profit growth stood at 44.35% for the Group and 44.05% for the Bank.

Key drivers: Strong growth in Net Interest Income (NIM improved by 24 bps to 4.51%), 22.05% growth in net fee and commission income, and a decline in impairment charges. The Cost-to-Income ratio (excluding taxes) improved to 29.66% from 33.94%.

Performance of Bangladesh operations and subsidiaries: Bangladesh operations contributed 15.72% of consolidated assets and 30.93% of consolidated profit before taxes.
